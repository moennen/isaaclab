RECOMMENDATIONS FOR OBSERVATION DESIGN
================================================================================

A. POTENTIALLY MISSING OBSERVATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------
  • cube_vel_xyz (3 dims): cube velocity would improve Markov completeness
    for post-contact dynamics. cube_z is tracked but not its velocity;
    cube_vel_z would help the policy detect whether the cube is falling.

  • dist_cube_xy (1 dim): horizontal distance from robot base.
    Derivable from (cube_x, cube_y) but making it explicit reduces policy
    burden for reachability classification.

  • ee_to_cube_xyz (3 dims): vector from EE to cube = cube_pos - ee_pos.
    This is the most direct input for approach_cube reward; high r expected.
    Currently the policy must infer it from separate cube_pos and ee_pos.

  • gripper_width (1 dim): continuous sum of finger joint positions
    (= f0 + f1, range 0..0.08). Currently only binary grip is in obs.
    Continuous width gives fine-grained grasp feedback unavailable from binary.

B. POSSIBLY REDUNDANT OBSERVATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------
  • If PCA 90% threshold uses ≤ 15 PCs (≤60% of dims), the joint space
    has significant correlations that could be reduced.
  • Slow-changing dims (lag-1 ≥ 0.98) add negligible new info per step;
    frame-stacking 2 steps captures dynamics without increasing obs size.

C. REWARD–OBSERVATION ALIGNMENT
------------------------------------------------------------
  • Dims with low Fisher ratio for BOTH splits (reachable/success) carry
    little discriminating information and may be prunable.
  • Low R² in reward predictability (within a label group) suggests the
    current obs cannot linearly predict the reward — either the reward is
    delayed (sparse) or depends on missing derived features.

D. MARKOV COMPLETENESS
------------------------------------------------------------
  • Dims with ΔR² > 0.05 indicate that knowing obs[t-1] helps predict
    obs[t+1] beyond what obs[t] alone provides. The most common cause is
    missing velocity terms (position dims look non-Markovian when velocity
    is absent). If arm velocities do NOT appear in the non-Markov list,
    including joint_vel is sufficient for Markov completeness at this scale.
